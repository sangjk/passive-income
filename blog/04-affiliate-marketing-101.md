## What Is Affiliate Marketing?

Affiliate marketing is simple: you recommend a product or service to your audience, and if someone buys through your unique link, you earn a commission. It's performance-based marketing — you only get paid when a sale happens.

**The beauty of affiliate marketing:** your content works for you 24/7. A blog post you write today can generate commissions months or years later. A YouTube video can earn money while you sleep. That's the passive income magic.

---

## How Affiliate Marketing Works

```
You create content → Someone reads/watches → 
They click your affiliate link → They buy the product → 
You earn a commission (typically 5–50%)
```

Four parties are involved:

1. **The Merchant** — Company selling the product
2. **The Affiliate** — You, promoting the product
3. **The Network** — Platform connecting affiliates and merchants (sometimes)
4. **The Customer** — Person who buys through your link

## Step 2: Find Affiliate Programs

### Types of Affiliate Programs

**1. Affiliate Networks** (one account, many merchants)

| Network | Notable Merchants | Payment |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| Amazon Associates | Everything | 1–10% |
| ShareASale | 25,000+ merchants | Varies |
| Impact / PartnerStack | SaaS companies | Varies |
| CJ Affiliate | Major brands | Varies |
| Rakuten | Large retailers | Varies |
| ClickBank | Digital products | 25–75% |

**2. In-House Programs** (direct with the company)

Many companies run their own programs. Search "[company name] affiliate program" to find them. Examples: Shopify, ConvertKit, Teachable, Jasper AI.

**3. High-Ticket Programs**

Some programs pay $100–$1,000+ per sale. These are harder to sell but incredibly lucrative with even a few sales per month.

## Step 4: Create Affiliate Content That Converts

### Content Types That Drive Affiliate Sales

1. **Product Reviews** — Honest, detailed review of a single product
2. **Comparison Posts** — "Product A vs Product B: Which Is Better?"
3. **Best-of Lists** — "10 Best [Product Type] for [Use Case]"
4. **Tutorials/How-To** — Show how to use a product, include affiliate link
5. **Resource Pages** — "Tools I Use" or "Recommended Resources"
6. **Case Studies** — "How I achieved [result] using [product]"

### Anatomy of a High-Converting Affiliate Post

```
1. Compelling headline with keywords
    
2. Introduction that identifies reader's problem
    
3. Quick product overview (if review/comparison)
    
4. Detailed features and benefits (not just specs)
    
5. Your personal experience (crucial for trust)
    
6. Pros and cons (honesty boosts conversion)
    
7. Who it's for / who it's NOT for
    
8. Pricing and value comparison
    
9. Clear call to action with affiliate link
    
10. FAQ addressing common objections
```

## Affiliate Marketing Best Practices

### DO:

- ✅ Only promote products you've actually used and believe in
- ✅ Disclose your affiliate relationship (legally required in most countries)
- ✅ Focus on helping, not selling — solve problems first
- ✅ Track your links to know what converts
- ✅ Update old content regularly (product info changes)
- ✅ Deep-link to specific product pages, not just homepages

### DON'T:

- ❌ Promote anything and everything for a quick buck
- ❌ Hide your affiliate relationship
- ❌ Create thin content just for affiliate links
- ❌ Ignore your audience's trust — it's your most valuable asset
- ❌ Expect overnight results — affiliate income is a marathon
