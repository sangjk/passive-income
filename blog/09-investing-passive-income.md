The catch? You need capital to start. But the good news is that you can begin with surprisingly little — and compound growth does the heavy lifting over time.

## Passive Income Investment Vehicles

### 1. Dividend Stocks

**How it works:** You buy shares in companies that distribute a portion of their profits to shareholders as dividends — typically quarterly.

| Stock Type | Yield | Risk | Example |
|-----------|-------|------|---------|
| Dividend Aristocrats | 2–4% | Low–Medium | Coca-Cola, J&J, Procter & Gamble |
| High-yield stocks | 4–8% | Medium–High | REITs, energy companies |
| Dividend ETFs | 2–5% | Low | SCHD, VYM, VIG |

**What you need:** $500–$5,000 to feel meaningful income. At 4% yield, $10,000 = $400/year in dividends.

### 2. REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)

**How it works:** REITs own and operate income-producing real estate. By law, they must distribute 90%+ of taxable income to shareholders.

| REIT Type | What It Owns | Average Yield |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Residential | Apartment buildings | 3–5% |
| Commercial | Office buildings, retail | 4–7% |
| Healthcare | Hospitals, senior living | 5–8% |
| Data center | Server facilities | 2–4% |
| Mortgage | Real estate loans | 7–12% |
| Diversified | Mix of property types | 3–6% |

**Top REIT platforms:** Fundrise, RealtyMogul, Streitwise, or buy publicly traded REITs through any brokerage.

### 3. Bonds & Fixed Income

**How it works:** You lend money to a government or corporation. They pay you interest and return your principal at maturity.

| Bond Type | Yield | Risk | Minimum |
|-----------|-------|------|---------|
| US Treasury bonds | 3–5% | Very Low | $100 |
| Corporate bonds | 4–7% | Low–Medium | $1,000 |
| Municipal bonds | 2–4% (tax-free) | Low | $5,000 |
| High-yield ("junk") bonds | 6–10% | High | $1,000 |
| I-Bonds (inflation-protected) | Varies with CPI | Very Low | $25 |

### 4. Index Funds & ETFs

**How it works:** Instead of picking individual stocks, you buy a fund that tracks a broad market index. Many pay regular dividends.

| Fund | Focus | Approx. Yield |
|------|-------|--------------|
| VTI | Total US stock market | 1.4% |
| VOO | S&P 500 | 1.5% |
| SCHD | Dividend-focused | 3.5% |
| VYM | High-dividend stocks | 3.0% |
| BND | Total bond market | 3.5% |

### 5. High-Yield Savings & CDs

**How it works:** Deposit money in a bank account that pays interest. The simplest form of passive income.

| Vehicle | Current Rates (2026) | Liquidity |
|---------|---------------------|-----------|
| High-yield savings | 3–5% APY | Immediate |
| CDs (1-year) | 4–6% | Locked for term |
| Money market funds | 4–5% | 1–2 days |
| Treasury bills (short-term) | 4–5% | 4–52 weeks |

## How to Build Your Passive Income Portfolio

### The Ladder Strategy

```
Layer 1: SAFETY (High-yield savings + T-bills)
→ Emergency fund + short-term needs
→ Target: 3–6 months of expenses

Layer 2: INCOME (Dividend stocks + REITs + Bonds)
→ Generate regular cash distributions
→ Target: Start with yield-focused ETFs

Layer 3: GROWTH (Index funds + Growth stocks)
→ Long-term capital appreciation
→ Target: Compounding over decades
```

| Allocation | Investment | Purpose | Annual Income |
|-----------|-----------|---------|--------------|
| $2,000 (20%) | High-yield savings | Emergency fund | ~$80 |
| $3,000 (30%) | SCHD (dividend ETF) | Dividend income | ~$105 |

**Total annual passive income: ~$350**
**Monthly: ~$29**

It's not life-changing — yet. But $10,000 invested is $350/year you didn't have before. Scale that to $100,000 and it's $3,500/year. Scale to $500,000 and it's $17,500/year — a meaningful side income.

## Getting Started With Minimal Capital

### If You Have $0–$100

1. Open a brokerage account (Robinhood, Fidelity, Schwab — all free)
2. Buy 1 share of a dividend ETF (SCHD costs ~$80)
3. Enable dividend reinvestment (DRIP — automatically buys more shares)
4. Add $25–$50/month consistently

### If You Have $100–$1,000

1. Open a Roth IRA (Fidelity, Vanguard, Schwab)
2. Max contributions grow tax-free
3. Invest in a simple 3-fund portfolio: VTI + VXUS + BND
4. Set automatic monthly contributions

### If You Have $1,000–$10,000

1. Fully fund your emergency savings first (high-yield account)
2. Max Roth IRA contributions ($7,000/year in 2026)
3. Build a dividend portfolio in a taxable brokerage account
4. Reinvest all dividends during accumulation phase

## Common Investing Mistakes

1. **Waiting until you "know enough"** — Start small. Learn by doing. Even $10 invested teaches you more than 10 hours of research.
2. **Chasing yield** — A stock paying 12% dividend is often very risky. Sustainable 3–5% yields are better.
3. **Timing the market** — "Time in the market beats timing the market." Regular investing wins.
4. **Ignoring fees** — A 1% annual fee can cost you hundreds of thousands over decades.
5. **Not reinvesting dividends** — DRIP (dividend reinvestment) is the engine of compound growth.
6. **Panic selling** — Markets drop. They always recover. Selling locks in losses.
