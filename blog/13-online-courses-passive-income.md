## Step 1: Choose Your Course Topic

### The Course Topic Sweet Spot

Your course topic should sit at the intersection of:

1. **What you know** — Skills, experience, or knowledge you have
2. **What people want to learn** — Proven demand in the market
3. **What people will pay for** — Topics that solve costly problems or unlock income

### Validating Course Demand

| Method | What to Look For | Tool |
|--------|-----------------|------|
| **Search volume** | People searching for "[topic] course" or "how to [topic]" | Google Trends, Ubersuggest |
| **Existing courses** | Competitors selling similar courses (competition = demand) | Udemy, Skillshare, Teachable |
| **YouTube engagement** | Tutorials on your topic getting 10K+ views | YouTube search |
| **Reddit/Quora** | People asking questions you can answer | Reddit, Quora |
| **Pre-sale test** | Offer a "coming soon" waitlist and track signups | Gumroad or landing page |

## Step 2: Structure Your Course

### Course Design Framework

```
MODULE 1: Foundation
├── Lesson 1: Welcome + Course Overview
├── Lesson 2: What You'll Achieve
└── Lesson 3: Mindset/Setup

MODULE 2: Core Skills
├── Lesson 4: Technique 1
├── Lesson 5: Technique 2
└── Lesson 6: Technique 3

MODULE 3: Advanced Application
├── Lesson 7: Combining Techniques
├── Lesson 8: Real-World Project
└── Lesson 9: Common Mistakes

MODULE 4: Mastery & Next Steps
├── Lesson 10: Optimization
├── Lesson 11: Scaling/Advanced
└── Lesson 12: What's Next
```

### Content Formats to Mix

| Format | Best For | When to Use |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Screen recording** | Software tutorials, digital skills | Step-by-step demonstrations |
| **Talking head** | Personal connection, concepts | Introductions, mindset |
| **Slides + voiceover** | Frameworks, theory | Explaining models and principles |
| **Worksheet/exercise** | Practice, application | After teaching a technique |
| **Case study** | Real examples, proof | Showing the method in action |
| **Interview** | Expert perspectives | Adding variety and credibility |

## Step 4: Choose Your Platform

| Platform | Fees | Best For |
|----------|------|----------|
| **Teachable** | $0–$299/mo + 5% | Full-featured course hosting |
| **Thinkific** | $0–$199/mo | Similar to Teachable |
| **Podia** | $39–$199/mo | Courses + digital products + community |
| **Gumroad** | 10% per sale | Simplest setup, lowest barrier |
| **Udemy** | 50% (organic) / 97% (affiliate) | Built-in audience |
| **Skillshare** | Based on watch time | Passive from existing users |
| **Kajabi** | $149–$399/mo | All-in-one marketing platform |
| **Your own site** | Hosting + Stripe (2.9%) | Full control, no platform fees |

### Platform Decision Guide

- **Fastest launch:** Gumroad (upload videos, set price, share link)
- **Built-in audience:** Udemy or Skillshare (lower revenue per sale, but they bring traffic)
- **Full control:** Teachable or Thinkific (you own the relationship and get full revenue)
- **Premium experience:** Kajabi (when you're doing $5K+/month)

## Step 5: Price Your Course

### Pricing by Course Depth

| Course Type | Length | Price Range |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| **Mini-course** | 30–60 min | $27–$97 |
| **Standard course** | 2–5 hours | $97–$297 |
| **Flagship course** | 5–10+ hours | $297–$997 |
| **Premium/cohort** | Live + recorded | $997–$5,000+ |

### Pricing Strategies

1. **Launch discount** — $197 at launch, raises to $297 after 100 students
2. **Payment plans** — $297 one-time or 3x$109 ($327 total, lower barrier)
3. **Bundle strategy** — Course + templates + community access = higher price
4. **Early bird pricing** — Pre-sell at 50% off before course is complete
