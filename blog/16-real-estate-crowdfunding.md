## What Is Real Estate Crowdfunding?

Real estate crowdfunding pools money from many investors to fund property acquisitions, developments, or loans. You own a fractional share of real estate assets and receive proportional returns — typically through dividends (rental income) and appreciation when properties sell.

---

## Types of Real Estate Crowdfunding

### 1. Equity REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)

| Feature | Public REITs | Private REITs (Fundrise, etc.) |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| **Liquidity** | Trade daily on stock exchange | Quarterly redemption windows |
| **Minimum** | Price of 1 share (~$10–$100) | $10–$1,000 |
| **Returns** | 3–8% dividend + appreciation | 4–12% dividend + appreciation |
| **Volatility** | High (market-driven) | Lower (appraisal-based) |
| **Fees** | None (brokerage) | 0.15–1.0% annual |

### 2. Debt / Private Lending

You lend money to real estate developers. They pay interest and return principal.

| Feature | Typical Terms |
|---------|--------------|
| **Returns** | 7–12% annual |
| **Term** | 6–24 months |
| **Security** | Secured by property lien |
| **Risk** | Default risk (mitigated by collateral) |

### 3. Individual Property Investments

Invest directly in a specific property alongside other investors. Higher minimums, more control.

| Feature | Typical Terms |
|---------|--------------|
| **Minimum** | $5,000–$50,000+ |
| **Returns** | 8–20% (higher risk/reward) |
| **Term** | 3–10 years |
| **Liquidity** | None until property sells |

## Risks to Understand

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| **Illiquidity** | Don't invest money you'll need within 3–5 years |
| **Market downturn** | Diversify across property types and geographies |
| **Platform failure** | Spread investments across 2–3 platforms |
| **Interest rate sensitivity** | REITs can decline when rates rise |
| **Development risk** | Stick to stabilized properties, not ground-up development |
| **Fee drag** | Compare all-in fees across platforms |
