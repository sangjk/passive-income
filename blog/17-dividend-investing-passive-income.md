## Dividends: The Original Passive Income

When you own dividend-paying stocks, companies literally send you money — just for being a shareholder. Every quarter, like clockwork, dividends arrive in your brokerage account. No work required. No decisions to make. It's the definition of passive income.

And with dividend reinvestment (DRIP), your passive income machine compounds on autopilot — buying more shares that generate more dividends that buy more shares.

## Key Dividend Concepts

### Dividend Yield

```
Dividend Yield = Annual Dividend per Share / Share Price

Example: $4 annual dividend / $100 share price = 4% yield
```

### Dividend Payout Ratio

```
Payout Ratio = Dividends Paid / Net Income

Healthy: 40–60% (company has room to grow dividends)
Risky: 80%+ (dividend might be cut)
```

### Dividend Growth Rate

The annual percentage increase in dividend payments. A stock yielding 3% with 10% annual dividend growth will double your income in ~7 years (rule of 72).

## The DRIP Strategy (Dividend Reinvestment)

DRIP automatically uses your dividends to buy more shares — including fractional shares. This is the engine of compound growth.

### DRIP in Action

Starting with $10,000 in SCHD (3.5% yield, 8% dividend growth, 7% price appreciation):

| Year | Portfolio Value | Annual Dividend Income |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| 0 | $10,000 | $350 |
| 5 | $17,100 | $560 |
| 10 | $29,300 | $900 |
| 15 | $50,100 | $1,450 |
| 20 | $85,700 | $2,320 |
| 25 | $146,700 | $3,720 |

From $350/year to $3,720/year — with zero additional investment. That's the power of DRIP compounding.

## Tax Strategy

| Account Type | Tax Treatment | Best For |
|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **Roth IRA** | Tax-free growth and withdrawals | Maximum tax efficiency |
| **Traditional IRA** | Tax-deferred growth, taxed on withdrawal | Current year tax deduction |
| **401(k)** | Tax-deferred | Workplace retirement |
| **Taxable brokerage** | Dividends taxed annually | Flexibility, no withdrawal restrictions |

**Priority:** Max Roth IRA first → 401(k) match → Taxable brokerage.

## Common Dividend Investing Mistakes

1. **Chasing yield** — A 10% yield usually signals trouble, not opportunity
2. **Ignoring payout ratio** — Above 80% is a red flag
3. **No diversification** — Don't put everything in one stock or sector
4. **Not reinvesting dividends** — DRIP is the compounding engine. Use it.
5. **Ignoring fees** — A 1% expense ratio costs $100/year on $10,000
6. **Selling in downturns** — Dividend investors benefit from lower prices (higher yield on reinvestment)
