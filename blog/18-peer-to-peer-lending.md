## Become the Bank: Earn Interest as a Lender

Peer-to-peer (P2P) lending cuts out traditional banks and lets you lend money directly to borrowers — earning interest income in return. You play the role of the bank, and you keep the profits.

P2P lending offers a unique combination: higher yields than savings accounts and bonds, monthly cash flow, and genuine passivity once your portfolio is set up.

---

## How P2P Lending Works

```
You deposit money on a P2P platform
         ↓
You select loans to invest in (or use auto-invest)
         ↓
Borrowers make monthly payments (principal + interest)
         ↓
You receive your share of payments in your account
         ↓
Reinvest returns to compound your income
```

## Building a Diversified P2P Portfolio

### Diversification Strategy

The #1 rule of P2P lending: diversify across many loans.

| Portfolio Size | Minimum Loans | Strategy |
|---------------|--------------|----------|
| $500 | 20+ ($25/loan) | Auto-invest, lowest risk grades |
| $1,000 | 40+ ($25/loan) | Auto-invest, mix of grades |
| $5,000 | 100+ ($50/loan) | Manual + auto, multiple grades |
| $10,000+ | 200+ ($50/loan) | Full grade diversification |

### Auto-Invest Settings

Most platforms offer auto-invest tools. Configure:

1. **Loan grade mix** — Spread across A through D grades
2. **Maximum per loan** — $25–$50 per note
3. **Term preference** — 36-month loans (lower default risk)
4. **Credit criteria** — Minimum credit score, max DTI
5. **Reinvestment** — Automatically reinvest repayments

## Risk Management

### Understanding the Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Default risk** | Borrower stops paying | Diversify across 100+ loans |
| **Platform risk** | Platform goes bankrupt | Use established platforms (10+ year history) |
| **Interest rate risk** | Better rates elsewhere | Shorter loan terms (36 months) |
| **Economic risk** | Recession increases defaults | Focus on higher-grade loans in downturns |
| **Liquidity risk** | Money locked in loans | Only invest what you won't need for 3–5 years |

### Grade Selection Strategy

| Loan Grade | Default Rate | Average Return | Strategy |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|----------|
| **A–B** | 1–3% | 4–6% | Conservative, steady income |
| **B–C** | 3–7% | 6–8% | Balanced, moderate growth |
| **C–D** | 7–12% | 8–10% | Growth-oriented |
| **D–E** | 12–20% | 10–15%+ | High risk, high reward |

## Common P2P Lending Mistakes

1. **Insufficient diversification** — 25 notes is the absolute minimum. 100+ is safer.
2. **Chasing the highest yields** — Grade E/F loans offer 15%+ but 20% default. Net return often lower than Grade B/C.
3. **Ignoring auto-invest** — Manual selection doesn't outperform auto-invest at scale.
4. **Not reinvesting** — Cash sitting idle earns 0%. Keep money working.
5. **Platform concentration** — Spread across 2–3 platforms for redundancy.
