## Case Study 1: Building a $3,000/Month Freelance Design Business in 90 Days

### Background

- **Person:** Maya, 28, graphic designer at an agency
- **Starting point:** $0 side income, 2 hours/day available
- **Goal:** $2,000/month within 6 months
- **Actual result:** $3,000/month in 90 days

### Strategy

Maya chose **Graphic Design (Canva)** as her side hustle because she already had design skills but wanted to use simpler tools to work faster.

### Timeline

| Week | Action | Result |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Created 8 portfolio pieces, set up Fiverr + Instagram | 0 clients |
| 2 | Posted daily on Instagram, sent 20 DMs to local businesses | 1 client ($50) |
| 3 | Delivered first project, got 5-star review, sent 20 more DMs | 2 clients ($100) |
| 4 | Raised prices to $75/project, posted Fiverr gig update | 3 clients ($225) |
| 5–8 | Consistent Instagram posting, Fiverr reviews building | $400–$600/month |
| 9–12 | Landed 2 monthly retainer clients ($400 each) + Fiverr | $1,200–$1,500/month |
| 13–16 | Added social media management upsell, raised retainer to $600 | $2,000–$2,500/month |
| 17–20 | 4 retainer clients + project work | $3,000/month |

### What Worked

1. **DM outreach** — 15% response rate, 5% conversion rate
2. **Monthly retainers** — Predictable income, less client acquisition
3. **Upselling** — Added social media management for +50% revenue per client
4. **Instagram as portfolio** — Every post was both marketing and portfolio

### What Didn't Work

1. **Waiting for Fiverr clients** — Too slow at first; outreach was faster
2. **Pricing too low initially** — $25 projects weren't worth the time
3. **No contract initially** — One client ghosted after delivery

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total hours invested (90 days) | ~180 hours |
| Revenue per hour (final month) | ~$50/hour |
| Client acquisition cost | $0 (organic only) |
| Client retention rate | 75% (3 of 4 retainers stayed) |
| Average project value | $150 |

## Case Study 2: From Side Project to $5,000/Month SaaS with No-Code

### Background

- **Person:** James, 32, product manager
- **Starting point:** $0 side income, no coding skills
- **Goal:** Build a simple tool that earns passive income
- **Actual result:** $5,000/month MRR in 8 months

### Strategy

James used **No-Code App Building** (Bubble) to create a simple project management tool for freelance designers.

### The Problem He Solved

Freelance designers were using messy spreadsheets to track projects, clients, and invoices. Existing tools were too complex or too expensive.

### Timeline

| Month | Action | MRR |
|-------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Learned Bubble, built MVP | $0 |
| 2 | Launched beta, got 10 free users | $0 |
| 3 | Added paid tier ($9/mo), 3 converted | $27 |
| 4 | Product Hunt launch, 50 new signups | $135 |
| 5 | Added features based on feedback, 20 paying | $180 |
| 6 | SEO content strategy, 50 paying | $450 |
| 7 | Affiliate program, 80 paying | $720 |
| 8 | Featured in design newsletter, 150 paying | $1,350 |
| 9 | Annual plan option (discount), 200 paying | $1,800 |
| 10 | Team plan ($29/mo), 250 individual + 15 teams | $2,700 |
| 12 | 350 individual + 30 teams | $5,000 |

### What Worked

1. **Solving a specific problem** — Not "project management" but "project management for freelance designers"
2. **Free tier** — Users try before they buy, reduces friction
3. **User feedback loop** — Every feature came from real user requests
4. **Niche content marketing** — SEO articles targeting "freelance designer [problem]"

### What Didn't Work

1. **Building too many features early** — Users only used 3 of the 10 features
2. **Ignoring mobile** — 40% of users tried to access on mobile
3. **No email onboarding** — Users who didn't get value in first session churned

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Development cost | $0 (Bubble free tier, then $32/mo) |
| Marketing spend | $200 total (one small ad test) |
| Customer acquisition cost | ~$3 (organic) |
| Monthly churn rate | 5% |
| Average revenue per user | $14/month |

## Case Study 3: $4,000/Month with Affiliate Marketing on a Niche Blog

### Background

- **Person:** Priya, 35, stay-at-home parent
- **Starting point:** $0 side income, 3 hours/day while kids at school
- **Goal:** Replace part-time income ($1,500/month)
- **Actual result:** $4,000/month in 14 months

### Strategy

Priya built a **Blog / Newsletter** focused on home coffee brewing equipment, monetized primarily through **Affiliate Marketing**.

### Timeline

| Month | Action | Traffic | Income |
|-------|--------|---------|--------|
| 1–2 | Wrote 20 articles, basic SEO | 200 visits/mo | $0 |
| 3–4 | Added affiliate links, 30 more articles | 1,000 visits/mo | $30 |
| 5–6 | First Google rankings, 20 more articles | 3,000 visits/mo | $150 |
| 7–8 | Comparison articles ranking, 15 more articles | 8,000 visits/mo | $500 |
| 9–10 | Hit page 1 for key terms, 10 more articles | 15,000 visits/mo | $1,200 |
| 11–12 | Added email list, newsletter launch | 20,000 visits/mo | $2,000 |
| 13–14 | Email promotions, updated old posts | 25,000 visits/mo | $4,000 |

### What Worked

1. **Comparison articles** — "Breville vs. DeLonghi" converted at 8%
2. **Long-tail keywords** — "Best espresso machine under $500" = low competition, high intent
3. **Updating old posts** — Refreshing 2024 → 2026 dates boosted rankings
4. **Email list** — Affiliate emails converted at 5% vs. 1.5% on blog

### What Didn't Work

1. **Social media** — Pinterest drove traffic but low conversion
2. **Short articles** — 800-word posts didn't rank; 2,000+ words did
3. **Amazon only** — Switching to direct affiliate programs doubled commissions

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total articles written | 95 |
| Average time per article | 3–4 hours |
| Average affiliate commission | $15 per sale |
| Conversion rate (blog) | 1.5% |
| Conversion rate (email) | 5% |
| Top earning article | $800/month (espresso machine comparison) |
