# 🎬 Video Editing — Side Hustle Guide

## Overview

Short-form video editing for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. High demand, growing market, and excellent earning potential.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Difficulty** | ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium) |
| **Time to First $** | 3–7 days |
| **Earning Potential** | $500–$5,000/month |
| **Upfront Investment** | $0–$30/month (software) |
| **Best Platforms** | Fiverr, Upwork, Twitter/X, direct outreach |

---

## Step 1: Choose Your Tools

### Video Editing Software Comparison

| Software | Cost | Best For | Learning Curve |
|----------|------|----------|---------------|
| **CapCut** | Free | Short-form (TikTok, Reels) | Easy |
| **DaVinci Resolve** | Free | Professional editing | Medium |
| **Premiere Pro** | $23/mo | Industry standard | Medium–Hard |
| **Final Cut Pro** | $300 (one-time) | Mac users | Medium |
| **After Effects** | $23/mo | Motion graphics | Hard |

### Recommended Stack for Beginners

**CapCut** (free) for short-form content → **DaVinci Resolve** (free) for long-form

### Essential Skills to Learn

1. **Cutting & trimming** — Remove dead space, umms, and pauses
2. **B-roll insertion** — Add visual variety over talking heads
3. **Text overlays** — Captions, titles, call-to-actions
4. **Transitions** — Jump cuts, zoom, whip pan
5. **Audio mixing** — Background music, sound effects, voice leveling
6. **Color correction** — Basic color grading for consistency
7. **Export settings** — Correct resolution, frame rate, and codec

## Step 3: Build Your Portfolio

### Portfolio Strategy

1. **Edit 5–8 sample videos** in your chosen niche:
   - Take popular long videos and create 60-second clips
   - Add captions, transitions, and sound effects
   - Show before/after comparisons

2. **Post edits on your own social media** — Proves you understand the format

3. **Create a portfolio reel** — 60-second montage of your best work

### Where to Showcase

- **Instagram/TikTok** — Your edits ARE your portfolio
- **YouTube** — Portfolio reel + behind-the-scenes
- **Google Drive folder** — Easy to share with clients
- **Frame.io** — Professional review platform (free tier)

---

## Step 4: Find Clients

### Direct Outreach (Most Effective)

**Twitter/X Strategy:**
1. Find creators with 10K–100K followers
2. Edit one of their videos for free
3. DM them: "I edited your recent video into a Short — feel free to use it! I can do this weekly for you."

**YouTube Strategy:**
1. Find channels without Shorts
2. Offer to repurpose their long videos into Shorts
3. "You're sitting on a goldmine of short-form content"

### Platform Gigs

**Fiverr:**
- "I will edit TikTok/Reels/Shorts videos with captions and effects"
- Start at $10/video, scale with reviews

**Upwork:**
- Search: "video editor," "TikTok editor," "YouTube editor"
- Long-term contracts pay more than one-off gigs

### Pricing Models

| Model | Price | Best For |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Per video** | $15–$50 | Short-form |
| **Per minute** | $30–$100 | Long-form |
| **Monthly retainer** | $300–$1,000 | Ongoing clients (4–8 videos/month) |
| **Revenue share** | 10–20% of video revenue | Growing channels |

---

## Step 5: Deliver & Scale

### Workflow

1. **Receive footage** — Google Drive, Dropbox, or WeTransfer
2. **First edit** — Rough cut with all elements (2–4 hours for short-form)
3. **Client review** — Use Frame.io for timestamped feedback
4. **Revisions** — Max 2 rounds included
5. **Final delivery** — MP4 in required resolution + platform specs

### Scaling Strategies

1. **Create presets & templates** — Reusable captions, transitions, effects
2. **Batch editing** — Edit 4–8 videos in one session
3. **Hire assistant editors** — Outsource rough cuts, you do final polish
4. **Add related services** — Thumbnail design, script writing, content strategy
5. **Build an agency** — Multiple editors, you manage clients

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

1. **Over-editing** — Too many effects distract from the message
2. **Ignoring audio** — Bad audio kills good video; always level and mix
3. **No revision policy** — Unlimited revisions = unlimited scope creep
4. **Not backing up projects** — Always keep project files + exports
5. **Working without a brief** — Get clear expectations before starting
