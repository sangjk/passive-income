# 🔊 Voiceover & Audio — Side Hustle Guide

## Overview

Record voiceovers for videos, ads, audiobooks, and e-learning courses. Low competition niche with consistent demand.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Difficulty** | ⭐⭐ (Easy–Medium) |
| **Time to First $** | 3–7 days |
| **Earning Potential** | $500–$5,000/month |
| **Upfront Investment** | $50–$200 (microphone) |
| **Best Platforms** | Fiverr, Upwork, ACX, Voices.com |

## Step 1: Set Up Your Studio

### Equipment Guide

| Equipment | Budget Option | Pro Option | Priority |
|-----------|--------------|------------|----------|
| **Microphone** | Fifine USB ($30) | Rode NT1 ($270) | Must-have |
| **Pop filter** | $5–$10 foam cover | Stedman Proscreen ($40) | Must-have |
| **Headphones** | Any closed-back | Audio-Technica ATH-M50x ($150) | Must-have |
| **Audio interface** | Not needed (USB mic) | Focusrite Scarlett ($170) | Nice-to-have |
| **Acoustic treatment** | Blankets/pillows | Acoustic panels ($100+) | Important |
| **DAW (software)** | Audacity (free) | Reaper ($60) | Must-have |

### Recording Space Setup

1. **Choose a quiet room** — Minimal echo, no ambient noise
2. **Soft surfaces** — Closets work great (clothes absorb sound)
3. **Close the windows** — Eliminate outside noise
4. **Turn off AC/fans** — Even quiet ones are audible in recordings
5. **Use a reflection filter** — Behind the mic to reduce room echo

## Step 2: Develop Your Voice Skills

### Voice Types & Markets

| Voice Type | Best Markets | Rate Range |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Warm & Friendly** | E-learning, corporate, explainer videos | $50–$200/project |
| **Authoritative** | Documentaries, news, business | $100–$300/project |
| **Energetic** | Commercials, promos, gaming | $75–$250/project |
| **Conversational** | Podcasts, YouTube, social media | $50–$150/project |
| **Character/Variety** | Animation, gaming, audiobooks | $100–$500/project |

### Practice Exercises

1. **Read aloud daily** — 15 minutes of any text
2. **Record and review** — Listen back critically
3. **Practice pacing** — Vary speed for emphasis
4. **Master breath control** — No audible inhales
5. **Learn pronunciation** — Common industry terms, brand names

## Step 3: Create Your Demo Reel

### Demo Structure (60–90 seconds)

1. **Commercial** — 2–3 ad reads (15 sec each)
2. **Narration** — 1 educational/documentary clip (20 sec)
3. **Character** — 1–2 character voices (10 sec each)
4. **Conversational** — 1 casual, friendly read (15 sec)

### How to Create Your Demo

1. **Write scripts** — Use real commercial copy or create similar ones
2. **Record each segment** — Multiple takes, pick the best
3. **Edit together** — Seamless transitions, no music needed
4. **Master the audio** — Normalize to -3dB, remove noise
5. **Export as MP3** — 44.1kHz, 192kbps

## Step 4: Find Clients

### Platform Strategy

**Fiverr:**
- "I will record a professional voiceover for your project"
- Start at $5 per 100 words, increase with reviews

**ACX (Audiobook Creation Exchange):**
- Find audiobook projects, audition with your demo
- Pay: $50–$250 per finished hour or royalty share

**Voices.com / Voice123:**
- Professional voiceover marketplaces
- Higher rates, more competition

**Upwork:**
- Search for "voiceover," "narration," "audiobook"
- Long-term clients possible

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

1. **Poor audio quality** — A great voice with bad audio won't get hired
2. **No demo reel** — Clients need to hear you before they buy
3. **Underpricing** — Don't race to the bottom; quality commands premium rates
4. **Ignoring hydration** — Drink water before recording; dry mouth = mouth clicks
5. **No contract** — Always define usage rights and revision limits
